Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 17, 2026

Fluxcode Studio LLC ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") operates the Checkpoint backup software and the website at checkpoint.fluxcode.studio (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your choices.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Contact information — If you contact us via email, GitHub issues, or a contact form, we collect your name, email address, and message content.
  • Newsletter — If you subscribe to our newsletter, we collect your email address.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Website analytics — We may collect standard web analytics data (pages visited, referrer, browser type, IP address) through privacy-respecting analytics tools. We do not use invasive tracking or sell data to third parties.
  • Error reports — If you opt in to error reporting, anonymized crash data may be sent to us.
  • Local storage — Our website uses browser localStorage to remember UI preferences (e.g., whether you have dismissed the newsletter popup). This data is stored only on your device and is never sent to our servers.

1.3 Third-Party Data Processors

Our website uses the following third-party services that may process limited data:

Service Purpose Data Shared
Formspree Contact form handling Name, email address, message content
Newsletter provider Email delivery Email address
GitHub Source code hosting, downloads Standard request data when you visit or download

Each service operates under its own privacy policy. Fonts are self-hosted and do not involve third-party requests.

1.4 Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Your backup data — Checkpoint runs entirely on your local machine. Your source code, files, databases, encryption keys, and backup contents never leave your device unless you configure cloud sync to your own storage provider. We have zero access to your backup data.
  • Telemetry — Checkpoint does not phone home, transmit usage statistics, or send any data to Fluxcode Studio servers during normal operation.
  • Cloud credentials — If you configure cloud sync (Dropbox, iCloud, S3, etc.), your credentials are stored locally on your machine. We never see or store them.

2. How We Use Information

  • To respond to your inquiries and support requests
  • To send newsletter updates (only if you opted in)
  • To improve the Service and fix bugs
  • To comply with legal obligations

3. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share information only:

  • With service providers who help us operate (e.g., email delivery), bound by confidentiality agreements
  • If required by law, subpoena, or legal process
  • To protect the rights, property, or safety of Fluxcode Studio LLC, our users, or the public

4. Data Retention

We retain contact information and support correspondence for as long as necessary to provide support and comply with legal obligations. Newsletter subscribers can unsubscribe at any time, after which we delete their email address within 30 days.

5. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access (GDPR Art. 15) — Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification (GDPR Art. 16) — Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to Erasure (GDPR Art. 17) — Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Data Portability (GDPR Art. 20) — Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Object (GDPR Art. 21) — Object to processing of your data for certain purposes
  • Right to Restrict Processing (GDPR Art. 18) — Request limitation of processing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — Where processing is based on consent, withdraw at any time
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint — File a complaint with your local data protection authority

For California residents (CCPA): You have the right to know what personal information we collect, request deletion, and opt out of any sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at . We will respond within 30 days.

6. Security

We use commercially reasonable measures to protect information transmitted to us. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website contains links to third-party websites (GitHub, cloud storage providers, etc.). We are not responsible for their privacy practices. See Section 1.3 for details on third-party services that process data on our behalf. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to individuals under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. Your continued use of the Service after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
